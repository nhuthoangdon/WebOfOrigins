About the Data Visualisation

Interactions & Data Interpretation

The network visualises connections between consumer products, their direct ingredients/compositions, raw materials, industries, and resulting social/environmental impacts, together with the associated countries in hierarchical order (vertically). Nodes represent entitites; edges show relationships. Hover over nodes and edges to see additional info. The larger the nodes, the more connections they have with other nodes ‚Üí the more prominent the entities are in the supply chain.

Use the control panel on the right of the network canvas to find keywords and toggle views (e.g., show/hide countries).

Use your mouse or the controls on the bottom right corner to manipulate the view (e.g. zoom in/out, focus)

Clicking on a node will highlight its direct connections (up to 2 hierarchy levels), trace the supply chain by clicking on the next relevant nodes one by one. Please note that multi-selection is currently not working.

Highlighted Nodes can be clicked and dragged for better view

Data Sources

The data used to construct the network is collected through extensive research. All supposely "claims" are based on credible sources cited in References. Data is not exhausive and might not cover all related entries in a pathway.

For countries, only the most prominent ones (usually the global top 3-5) are featured. Country Roles (e.g. Producer, Consumer, Importer, Exporter) are paired with relative adjectives of importance (e.g. significant importer, major consumer) and not ranking as a country's production data can change constantly. Occasionally, a country might be listed as "the largest" or "global leader" if its role is stable and the data is consistent across various sources.
