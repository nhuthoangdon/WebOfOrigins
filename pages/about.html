Beyond the surface: From origins to impacts in an interconnected world

Today’s consumer goods—from your morning coffee to medications to household items — emerge from vast, interconnected industrial webs. Many of their ingredients and materials overlap across sectors, creating shared environmental and social impacts.

Whether you’re fighting deforestation, ensuring your daily essentials are sourced from sustainable ingredients, or simply curious about what’s inside your vitamin capsules, understanding the root causes matters.

Who would imagine that coffee could be linked to deforestation in the Amazon, that supplements might contain fish oil from overfished species, that photographic film relied on gelatin made from animal hides and bones, or that life-saving blood thinners might depend on compounds derived from pigs? The industrial supply chain is large, complex, and tangled. A product might contain a wide range of ingredients, many of which could come from a questionable origin. Understanding product pathways is a good start towards consumer awareness and addressing the right issues.

The Web of Origins (WOO) Project stems from this very curiosity, an investigation into the origins of everyday products and their compositions. It began as a quest to discover what things are made of, where they come from, and how seemingly unrelated items are connected. Ultimately, it serves as a reference point for addressing and improving issues tied to supply chains, sustainability, environmental impacts, ethics, and even social justice.

Transparency: a new approach to consumerism and activism

WOO's ambition is to catalog a wide range of products and their sources, exploring the entangled web of industrialisation and seeks to reveal how products and industries are directly or indirectly linked to social and environmental impacts, from ethical concerns and human well-being to ecological sustainability. By making these connections publicly visible, WOO promotes transparency and empowers people to make informed choices and encourages a deeper understanding of the world’s interconnected industrial systems and supply chains.

Fuel the mission
